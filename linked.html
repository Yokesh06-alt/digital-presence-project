Introduction to AI

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.

These include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and natural language understanding.

AI is revolutionizing industries by automating processes, enabling smart assistants, and helping with medical diagnostics.

History of AI

AI has roots in philosophy and math. The 1956 Dartmouth Conference marked its formal beginning. Growth and setbacks followed in waves called "AI winters."

Applications of AI

  • Natural Language Processing
  • Computer Vision
  • Robotics
  • Healthcare
  • Autonomous Vehicles

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: Specialized for specific tasks.
  • General AI: Human-level across domains.
  • Super AI: Beyond human capabilities.

The Future of AI

AI is evolving rapidly, integrating into life and work, but bringing ethical concerns like bias, privacy, and job disruption.

Fields like explainable AI and human-AI collaboration are shaping the path forward.
